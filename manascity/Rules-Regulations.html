rules & regulation

Admission

Schedule

For LKG Class, admission starts in the last week of January. Admission for other classes upto XI, depending on available vacancy, starts in the last week of March

Eligibilty
  • To be eligible for admission to LKG class, the candidate should be three & half year and above.
  • For admission to other classes, the candidate should have passed the previous class from a recognised school.
Procedure
  • Candidate will apply for admission on the prescribed form along with the photocopy of passed Report Card of previous class.
  • On the basis of test and interview candidates will be shortlisted.
  • For proof of date of birth, they will produce certificates signed by the competent authority/ Nagar Nigam or the transfer certificates from recognised schools.
  • The selected candidates are required to bring their two latest passport size coloured photographs & One family photograph (father & mother only) of size (3.5 c.m. X 4.5 c.m.) at the time of Admission.
  • Transfer Certificates duly countersigned by the Educational Authorities of the district in which the previous school is located, will be submitted at the time of admission.
  • No admission form will be entertained without the submission of the photocopy of passed Report-Card of previous class as well as the Transfer Certificate of the last school attended.
  • No change in Student’s Name, Father’s Name, Mother’s Name and Date of Birth of a scholar will be allowed in the records of the institution.
Attendance
  • Every student will regularly and punctually attend the school.
  • Late comers will not be allowed to attend classes without the written permission of the Principal.
  • For absence due to unavoidable reasons, students will submit written application duly signed by their parents. In case of illness, medical certificate given by a registered doctor will be attached.
  • Leave applications must be submitted within three days of absence.
    For continuous absence for ten days without permission, student’s names will be struck off the school rolls. The Principal however, may allow re-admission.
  • A student may apply for a Transfer Certificate on payment of the prescribed fee.

Co-curricular Activities

STUDENT GOVERNANCE HIERARCHY

  • SCHOOL CAPTAIN
  • SCHOOL VICE CAPTAIN
  • HOUSE CAPTAIN
  • HOUSE VICE CAPTAIN
  • SPORTS INCHARGE
  • SANITARY INCHARGE
  • CULTURAL INCHARGE
  • DISCIPLINE INCHARGE

To develop among students capability of organizing co-curricular activities, we have divided each School into five Committees: Action Service, Good Moments, Personality Development, Beautification, E-Committee.

Each Committee works to the goals of their committee in their own classes. They also work vertically across grades and meet once a week on Saturdays with their teacher mentors, for classes 3 to 6 and for classes 7 to 11.

Literary / Cultural activities and games/ sports are conducted class and school wide basis throughout the year to provide opportunity to all students, with the view to develop their leadership skills and team work spirit. Activities are conducted according to our philosophy of 'Comptete with Yourself' with focus on cooperative games.
